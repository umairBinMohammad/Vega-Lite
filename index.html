Inferno of Black Saturday: Victoria's Devastating Bushfires

A Visual Chronicle of the Catastrophic Fires that Shaped Australia in 2009

  • On February 7, 2009, Victoria experienced one of the most devastating bushfire events in Australian history. Known as Black Saturday, these fires resulted in significant loss of life, property, and wildlife. This visualization aims to provide an overview of the bushfire occurrences, their intensity, and the economic impact on the region.

Bushfires burned bright across Victoria

  • The disaster impacted communities, ecosystems, and the economy, leaving a lasting mark on the region. This visualization highlights the scale and impact of the fires across Australia in 2009.

Post-Bushfire Reconstruction and Recovery Efforts

  • The aftermath of Black Saturday triggered a monumental effort in rebuilding homes, communities, and ecosystems. Government spending surged, directing resources toward infrastructure, public facilities, and emergency services.
  • This increase in government expenditure is reflected in the economic activity data, where construction and recovery efforts significantly boosted Victoria's State Final Demand (SFD).
